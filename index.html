English Listening Practice

Improve your listening skills from A1 to C1

Level A1 (Beginner)

Focus: Survival English and very basic phrases. Simple and everyday vocabulary.

  • Hello! My Basic World: greetings, farewells, introducing yourself (name, age, nationality), immediate family, and household objects.
  • My Daily Life: simple routines (wake up, eat, study/work), days of the week, telling the time, and basic leisure activities.
  • Where Is It?: city places (store, park, bank), simple directions, food and drinks for ordering, and colors.

Level A2 (Elementary)

Focus: Communicating past and future experiences and describing with more detail.

  • My Past and My Surroundings: simple biography, describing your town and comparing it, weather and seasons.
  • Likes, Plans, and Experiences: hobbies, preferences, weekend plans, vacations, telling past stories.
  • Daily Tasks and Health: shopping (clothes, prices, sizes), ordering food, describing symptoms and parts of the body.

Level B1 (Intermediate)

Focus: Starting, maintaining, and ending conversations. Expressing opinions and giving advice.

  • Stories and Personal Experiences: narrating anecdotes, achievements, dreams, and life experiences.
  • Opinions and Advice: giving points of view on daily topics, expressing agreement/disagreement.
  • Culture and Lifestyle: comparing customs, habits, and discussing future professional goals.

Level B2 (Upper-Intermediate)

Focus: Fluency in abstract topics, structured arguments, and more refined language.

  • Debates and Arguments: advantages/disadvantages, defending a position, expressing hypotheses.
  • Relationships and Communication: discussing different types of relationships, non-verbal language, idioms.
  • Culture and Society in Depth: trends, media criticism, ethics, and values.

Level C1 (Advanced)

Focus: Mastery of the language with precision and awareness of nuances. Idiomatic and specialized vocabulary.

  • Abstract and Specialized Topics: philosophy, science, globalisation, and expert fields.
  • Figurative Language and Humor: idioms, wordplay, cultural humor, sarcasm.
  • Critical Analysis and Persuasion: persuasive presentations, literary/text analysis, rhetorical strategies.
